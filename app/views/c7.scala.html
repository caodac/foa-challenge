So many associations to test, so little money ...

By amassing a team of motivated grad students and a lot of coffee, a group has amassed a rich dataset of 1,000,000 gene-disease associations. The data is available here and lists the gene, disease it is associated with and the probability of the association.

Now the group is looking for funding to validate a subset of these associations. The group is interested in identifying the subset of associations such that

  1. the selected associations include every gene and every disease from the original dataset (there may be repetitions of a gene or disease), and
  2. the sum of the probabilities of the selected associations is maximized ( correct to the second decimal place)

Can you help them? Your answer should be a CSV file in the form 

"Gene","Disease","Prob"
"G1","D1",PROB

where the first line is the header, and then each row is an association between a gene and a disease and the given probability. Note that all text fields should be quoted using double quotes.
